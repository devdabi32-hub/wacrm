Design Notes — Variant A: Poster Grid

Visual Feel
Product catalogue / CMS for travel brand. Makes use of destination images (poster_url). Cards feel like a booking platform's admin.
Best For
Clients who want to show destinations with photos. Strong visual impact at first glance — great for demo / sales.
Pros
✓ Modern, premium look. Destinations feel like products.
✓ poster_url images shine here.
✓ Instantly client-presentable.
Cons
✗ Less efficient when 20+ destinations (scrolling).
✗ Without poster images, cards look sparse.

Design Notes — Variant B: Power Table

Visual Feel
Dense, efficient, business-like. Feels like Linear, Vercel's deployment list, or Supabase dashboard. Every pixel earns its place.
Best For
Power users with 20+ destinations. Easy to scan, compare prices, toggle active status in bulk. Most productive for day-to-day management.
Pros
✓ Fastest to scan and act on 20+ items.
✓ Price/duration columns easy to compare.
✓ Edit button always visible — no 3-dot needed.
Cons
✗ Less visual impact for client demos.
✗ Doesn't showcase destination images at all.

Design Notes — Variant C: Enhanced List

Visual Feel
Rich detail without going full grid. Like Framer's asset library or Notion's list view. More info per row, actions visible inline.
Best For
Best balance of visual richness + information density. Shows keywords (AI context), departure dates, and summary all at once. Best for day-to-day management AND client demos.
Pros
✓ Most information visible per row.
✓ Keywords visible — shows AI context.
✓ Inline Edit button, no 3-dot hunting.
✓ Scales well from 5 to 50 destinations.
Cons
✗ Taller rows than table variant.
✗ Without poster images, left side looks plain.
